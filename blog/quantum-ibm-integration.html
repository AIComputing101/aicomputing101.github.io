Quantum-101 Gets IBM Quantum Integration

We're excited to announce a major update to our quantum-computing-101 repository: full integration with IBM Quantum hardware! This means you can now take the quantum algorithms you've been experimenting with in simulators and run them on real quantum processors.

What's New?

The update includes:

  • A step-by-step guide to setting up IBM Quantum Experience access
  • Modified circuit templates optimized for physical quantum hardware
  • Noise mitigation techniques specific to IBM's quantum processors
  • Jupyter notebooks comparing simulator vs. real hardware results

Why This Matters

While simulators are great for learning, real quantum hardware behaves differently due to noise, decoherence, and physical constraints. This integration helps bridge the gap between theoretical quantum computing and practical implementation — a critical skill for anyone looking to work with quantum technologies.

Getting Started

To get started with the new integration:

  1. Create a free account on IBM Quantum Experience
  2. Clone the latest version of quantum-computing-101
  3. Follow the setup guide in docs/ibm-quantum-setup.md
  4. Run the example notebooks in examples/ibm-quantum/

Community Challenge

We're launching a community challenge to celebrate this update! Submit your optimized quantum circuit (using our new templates) that achieves the lowest error rate on IBM's ibmq_lima processor. The top 3 submissions will be featured in our next blog post and get a shoutout in our repository.

Happy quantum coding! As always, we welcome contributions — whether it's improving our hardware-optimized circuits, adding new noise mitigation techniques, or sharing your own experiments.
